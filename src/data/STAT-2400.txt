5.1 Basic Definition (Weiss §8.1)
Definition 5.1. Continuous Random Variable
A random variable is called a continuous random variable if
P(X = x) = 0 for all x 2 R X .
I Notes:
• The range R X of a continuous random variable X is infinite and uncountable. (Otherwise,
X would be discrete!)
In other words, R X is an interval of the real line or the union of many such intervals.
• Classical examples are: heights, weights, waiting times, etc. (measurements of some kind).
• Unlike what we did in the discrete setup, for any given value x 2 R X , the probability that
X = x is not what we will be interested in. (It is zero!)
Rather, we will be interested in the probability that X is close to x, that is, something like
P(x - h  X  x + h) or P(x  X  x + h) for some small h > 0.
• Some random variables are not discrete nor continuous.

Question 1:
Assume that someone is to roll a six-sided die 10 times.
(A) What is the probability that at least one result of six is obtained in the 10 die rolls?
(B) What is the probability that no fives and sixes are obtained in the 10 die rolls?
(C) Knowing that the first six is obtained on the 7th roll of the die, calculate the probability
that 2 sixes are obtained in total.
Question 2:
In a shootout, Jonathan and Mario take turns trying to beat the goalkeeper to score a goal.
The shootout is done in successive rounds where each player gets one attempt at scoring.
Specifically, in the first round, Jonathan and Mario each get one attempt. If only one player
scores, he is declared the winner and the shootout ends. If both players score on their
attempt, or if both players don’t score, they move on to a second round. This is repeated
until a player is declared the winner.
Assume that, in any round, the probabilities that Jonathan and Mario score a goal are,
respectively, 2/3 and 3/4. Finally, let X denote the number of rounds needed to end the
shootout.
(A) Justify that X has a geometric distribution with parameter p = 5/12.
(B) What are the mean and variance of X?
(C) Jonathan and Mario engage in a shootout every day of the week. What are the mean
and variance of the total number of rounds played over the week?
